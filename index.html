    Public bike sharing programs are a major aspect to many urban cities' biking infrastructure. Toronto has Bike Share Toronto, Montreal has BIXI, and Vancouver has Mobi.

    All 3 cities' bike sharing programs operate with a docking model, where stations are placed around the city at which users rent and return bikes.

    Since users are able rent a bike from one dock and return it to any other dock, major components of the usability of a bike share network are station coverage and distribution.

    Now that we've done some of our own investigating, let's see what the experts in the field are saying.

    People for Bikes is an organization dedicated to promoting bicycling as a safe and accessible mode of transportation. Every year, People for Bikes releases a city ranking, where each city is given a Bicycle Network Analysis (BNA) score.

    According to the 2024 BNA scores, Toronto ranks 262th out of 2390 North American cities with a score of 47. This is behind other major Canadian cities, Montreal with 71 and Vancouver with 66.

    BNA scores act as an indicator for how well a city's bike network connects people with places they want to go. This score is calculated based on 6 metrics, People, Opportunity, Core Services, Recreation, Retail, and Transit.
